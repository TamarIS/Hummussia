Our Story

Rooted in tradition · Grown in Berlin

The Long Way to the Right Thing

Navot Shelach took the scenic route. Degrees in Psychology and Business, years of wondering what it all adds up to — until he stepped into a kitchen and everything clicked. He went to culinary school and came out top of his class. For the next twenty years he cooked his way up: pizzaiolo, grill cook, head chef at a restaurant group that made real waves in Israel's food scene.

Then he walked away. Because after two decades in fine kitchens, he realised the food that moved him most was never the complicated kind. It was his grandmother's hummus. Workers' food. The stuff that actually tastes like home. He calls it Hummus Culture — no stuffy rules, no performance, just honest flavour and a table worth sitting at.

He did pop-ups, opened hummus spots, and eventually landed in Berlin at Kedem, where he spent a year feeding the city and falling in love with it. Now he's channelling twenty years of craft into something simple and true: die Hummussia. Everything he learned in fancy restaurants, poured into a bowl of hummus. Come hungry. Leave better.
